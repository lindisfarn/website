# Lindisfarn — Ideal Customer Avatar

> **Purpose**: A psychological profile of our perfect customer to guide all copywriting, messaging, and brand voice decisions.

---

## At a Glance

| Attribute | Profile |
|-----------|---------|
| **Name** | Sarah (or Michael) |
| **Age** | 34–48 |
| **Location** | Ottawa, Montreal, or rural Ontario (within 2hr of Glen Robertson) |
| **Household** | 2 adults + 1–3 children |
| **Income** | $120K–$250K household |

## Demographics

### Family Structure
- Married or partnered, typically with young children (ages 2–12)
- May have one partner who works from home or has flexible hours
- Often the **primary grocery decision-maker** is the mother, but the father is actively involved in food philosophy discussions

### Geographic
- Lives in a **suburb or semi-rural area** within driving distance of Ottawa/Montreal
- May already be familiar with farmers' markets or CSAs
- Values being "close to the land" even if they don't farm themselves

### Economic
- Comfortable but not extravagant—can afford premium food but still makes conscious choices
- Already spends **$200–$400/month** on quality groceries beyond basics
- Sees food as an investment in health, not an expense to minimize

---

## Psychographics

### Core Identity Beliefs
1. **"We are what we eat."** They believe food quality directly impacts physical health, mental clarity, and even mood.
2. **"I'm raising my children differently."** Rejects the idea that convenience should trump quality. Wants kids to know where food comes from.
3. **"I see through the marketing."** Distrusts grocery store labels like "natural," "free-range," and "organic" because they've learned these terms are largely meaningless.
4. **"Voting with my dollars."** Believes purchasing choices are moral choices. Feels good supporting local, ethical producers.

### Values (Ranked)
1. **Health & vitality** — Physical wellbeing is non-negotiable
2. **Authenticity** — Has a strong BS detector; hates corporate greenwashing
3. **Family** — Decisions are made for the children's future
4. **Community** — Prefers to know the face behind the product
5. **Sustainability** — Cares about the land, but primarily through the lens of personal health

### Lifestyle Markers
- Reads Substack newsletters, listens to podcasts (health, parenting, alternative education)
- May homeschool or use alternative schooling (Montessori, Waldorf, forest school)
- Does yoga, walks, or runs—not hardcore fitness, but consistent movement
- Has a garden (or wants one) and may already raise a few backyard chickens
- Uses cast iron, not Teflon
- Has strong opinions about seed oils

## Pain Points & Frustrations

### About Industrial Food
- **"I can't trust what I'm buying."** Even at Whole Foods, they feel deceived.
- **"It all tastes the same."** Remembers what chicken used to taste like at grandma's house. Today's chicken is watery and flavorless.
- **"I don't know where to find the real thing."** Has looked for pasture-raised poultry but found it confusing, inconsistent, or too far away.

### About Their Lifestyle
- **Time-poor.** Wants to do the right thing but doesn't have hours to research every purchase.
- **Overwhelmed by options.** Too many certifications, too many claims, too much noise.
- **Guilt.** Knows they should do better but falls back on convenience more than they'd like.

### About Buying Direct
- **Fear of commitment.** Doesn't want to be locked into a subscription or CSA they can't control.
- **Fear of waste.** Worried about buying too much and letting it spoil.
- **Logistical concerns.** How will it be delivered? Will it stay frozen?

---

## Desires & Aspirations

### What They Want to Feel
- **Peace of mind.** Want to put food on the table without second-guessing it.
- **Pride.** Want to feel like they're doing right by their family.
- **Connection.** Want to feel part of something meaningful, not just a transaction.
- **Simplicity.** Want someone to just tell them: "Here. This is the good stuff."

### What They Want to Say
- *"We get our chicken from this incredible small farm…"* (social currency)
- *"You can actually taste the difference."* (validation of their choice)
- *"The farmer knows my name."* (relationship, not transaction)

### What They Want for Their Children
- Kids who understand where food comes from
- Kids who develop sophisticated palates
- Kids who grow up with a healthy relationship to food

---

## Objections & Hesitations

| Objection | Underlying Fear |
|-----------|-----------------|
| "It's too expensive." | Fear of judgment (from partner, from self) for spending "too much" on chicken |
| "I'm not sure I'll like it." | Fear of wasting money on something unfamiliar |
| "What if the farm goes under?" | Fear of losing a source they've grown to depend on |
| "I don't have time to pick it up." | Fear of adding complexity to an already busy life |

### How to Overcome
- **Price**: Frame as investment in health, not luxury expense. Compare to cost of supplements or doctor visits.
- **Taste**: Offer small trial order. Testimonials from similar families.
- **Reliability**: Emphasize the "Founding Families" commitment—you're building this together.
- **Logistics**: Make delivery seamless. Remove friction.

---

## Messaging Implications

### Voice & Tone
- **Warm but not folksy.** They're educated; don't talk down to them.
- **Confident but not preachy.** State the truth without lecturing.
- **Intimate but not overly familiar.** They're joining a relationship, not a cult.

### Words That Resonate
- Pasture, sunlight, soil, nourishment
- Real, honest, true
- Family, home, table
- Steward, covenant, care
- Slow, deliberate, seasonal

### Words to Avoid
- Organic (overused, distrusted)
- Sustainable (vague, corporate)
- All-natural (meaningless)
- Eco-friendly (sounds like marketing)
- Guilt-free (implies guilt)

### Proof Points That Matter
- **"moved to fresh pasture daily"** (specific = credible)
- **"heritage breeds raised for flavor"** (shows expertise)
- **"25 families, not 25,000"** (exclusivity, intimacy)
- **"no barn, no cages, no shortcuts"** (clarity of practice)

## A Day in Their Life

**6:15 AM** — Wakes before the kids. Makes pour-over coffee. Scrolls a health newsletter while the house is quiet.

**7:30 AM** — Breakfast with kids. Wishes she had better eggs. The ones from Costco are fine, but...

**12:00 PM** — Quick lunch between meetings. Eats a salad she meal-prepped. Feels virtuous but a little bored.

**5:30 PM** — Starts dinner. Opens the freezer and sees the chicken breasts. Sighs. Knows it's going to be bland. Adds more seasoning than she should have to.

**8:00 PM** — Kids in bed. Browses Instagram. Sees an ad for a farm offering pasture-raised poultry. Clicks. Reads. Feels a spark of hope. Bookmarks it.

**10:30 PM** — In bed, mentions to partner: "I found this farm that raises chickens the right way. Might be worth trying." Partner: "How much?" She doesn't know yet. Falls asleep thinking about it.

## Trigger Moments

These are the moments when Sarah is most likely to seek out (and buy) from Lindisfarn:

1. **After reading an article about poultry industry practices.** Disgust drives action.
2. **After tasting truly good chicken at a friend's house.** "Where did you get this?"
3. **After a health scare (hers, partner's, or child's).** Suddenly food quality becomes urgent.
4. **At the start of a new season (especially spring).** New Year's resolution energy, "eating clean" phase.
5. **When expecting a child or just after birth.** Heightened protectiveness over what enters the home.

---

## Summary: The One Sentence

> **Sarah is a health-conscious, skeptical, time-poor mother who wants to feed her family real food but is overwhelmed by noise and distrust. She's looking for a trusted source—a relationship, not a transaction—and is willing to pay a premium for peace of mind.**
